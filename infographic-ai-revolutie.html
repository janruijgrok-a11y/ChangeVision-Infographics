1. The Foundation: Where Does the Money Come From?

For years, the business model of tech giants was built on three pillars: targeted advertising, cloud computing, and hardware. But the dominance of one pillar is undeniable. This is the foundation that is now under pressure.

Revenue Sources of Big Tech

A simplified representation of the average business model. The focus on advertising is the Achilles' heel in an AI-driven world.

2. Mighty Entities: Bigger Than Countries

The economic scale of the largest tech companies is so gigantic that their annual revenue surpasses that of national economies. Their influence thus extends far beyond traditional markets.

Revenue vs. Gross Domestic Product (GDP)

A comparison of the annual revenue of tech giants with the GDPs of countries.

3. The Great Shift: From Oil to Data

Over the past two decades, the world has witnessed a fundamental shift in economic power. Traditional giants, represented by companies like General Electric and ExxonMobil, have lost their top positions to the rising tech giants. The value of data and network effects now outweighs the value of physical resources.
